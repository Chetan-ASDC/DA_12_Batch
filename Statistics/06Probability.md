# Types of Probability and Their Expression 

---

## What is Probability?
Probability means **chance**.  
It tells us **how likely something is to happen**.

Example:  
Chance of rain today, chance of winning a game, chance of getting heads.

---

## 1. Theoretical Probability

### Meaning 
Theoretical probability is used **when all outcomes are equally possible**.

---

### Formula
P(Event) = Favourable outcomes / Total outcomes

---

### Example
Tossing a coin:
- Outcomes: Head, Tail (2)
- Favourable outcome: Head (1)

P(Head) = 1/2

This is theoretical probability because both outcomes are equally likely.

---

## 2. Experimental Probability

### Meaning 
Experimental probability is based on **what actually happens in real life**.

---

### Formula
P(Event) = Number of times event occurs / Total trials

---

### Example
A coin is tossed 10 times:
- Head comes 6 times

P(Head) = 6/10 = 0.6

This probability is based on an experiment.

---

## 3. Subjective Probability

### Meaning 
Subjective probability is based on **personal thinking or experience**, not on calculation.

---

### Example
A teacher says:
"I think this exam will be easy."

This is subjective probability because it depends on opinion.

---

## 4. Axiomatic Probability

### Meaning 
Axiomatic probability follows **basic rules** of probability.

---

### Simple Rules
- Probability is always between 0 and 1  
- 0 means impossible  
- 1 means certain  

---

### Example
If the chance of rain is 0.8, then:
Chance of no rain = 1 − 0.8 = 0.2

---

## How Probability is Expressed

Probability can be written in three ways:

---

### 1. Fraction
Example:  
1/2

---

### 2. Decimal
Example:  
0.5

---

### 3. Percentage
Example:  
50%

---

## Final Summary 

- Probability means chance  
- Theoretical probability uses logic  
- Experimental probability uses real results  
- Subjective probability uses opinion  
- Axiomatic probability follows rules  
- Probability can be written as fraction, decimal, or percentage
